1. Use a Password Manager

Instead of remembering 50 passwords, use an app like Proton Pass. It creates long, random passwords (like xh9!kL2mP) and remembers them for you.

> INTERACTIVE STRENGTH TESTER:

Enter at least 8 characters...

> Rule: Aim for 12+ characters with symbols.

▼ TEST YOUR STRENGTH
2. Turn on Two-Factor (2FA)

2FA is your "Plan B." If a hacker steals your password, they still can't get in without the secret code sent to your phone or generated by an app.

> Layer 1: Password (Stolen)

> Layer 2: 6-Digit SMS Code (Blocked!)

> Result: Hacker is denied access despite having your password.

3. Check the URL (Link)

Before you type your password, look at the address bar. Make sure it says exactly "netflix.com" and not something weird like "netflix-verify.com."

> VERIFYING DOMAIN...

> Look for the Padlock icon.

> Check for "https://" which means your connection is encrypted.

4. Update Your Phone & Computer

Software updates aren't just for new features, they are "repairs" for security holes that hackers use to get into your device. Update as soon as you see the notification!

> Downloading security patches...

> Reason: Developers find bugs (zero-days) that hackers use. Updating closes those doors forever.

▼ VIEW PROTOCOL
5. Don't Click Under Pressure

If an email says "Your account will be DELETED in 10 minutes!" it is trying to panic you. If you feel rushed or scared, it's probably a scam. Take a breath and don't click.

> Analysis: Psychological manipulation detected.

> Scammers rely on urgency to bypass your common sense. Wait 10 minutes before reacting.

6. Use a VPN on Public Wi-Fi

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) acts like a "shield" for your internet. It hides what you are doing so people at the coffee shop can't see your data.

> VPN Tunnel Active...

> Your IP: [HIDDEN]

> Your Traffic: [ENCRYPTED]

7. Lock Your Private Accounts

Go into your Instagram or TikTok settings and make your account private. This stops random people and hackers from seeing your personal info.

> Privacy Wall: ENGAGED

> Visibility: Friends Only

> Metadata: Stripped from public view.

▼ VIEW PROTOCOL
8. Back Up Your Photos

Keep your important photos and schoolwork on a separate thumb drive or in the cloud (like Google Drive). If your computer ever gets a virus, you won't lose your memories.

> Backup Rule: 3-2-1

> 3 copies of data, 2 different formats, 1 copy kept offline.
