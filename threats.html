1. Fake Emails (Phishing)

This is when a hacker sends an email that looks like it's from Netflix, Amazon, or your Bank. They want you to click a link and type in your password so they can steal it.

[!] INCOMING TRAP:

FAKE: support@PayPa1.com

REAL: service@paypal.com

> Analysis: The "1" replaces the "l" to deceive your eyes.

2. Scary Pop-ups (Scareware)

Have you ever seen a window say "YOUR COMPUTER HAS 50 VIRUSES"? Usually, that window is the virus. They want you to click it so they can install bad software on your computer.

> SYSTEM_ALERT: "URGENT! 48 Threats Detected. Click to scan now."

> FACT: Websites cannot scan your hard drive. This is a mental trick to induce panic.

3. Public Wi-Fi Dangers

On an unencrypted Wi-Fi network, a hacker can sit "in the middle" of your connection. Everything you type—including passwords—passes through their computer before it reaches the website.

> Network Sniffing in progress...

> Unsecured WiFi allows anyone on the same router to "see" your raw data packets as they fly through the air.

▼ VIEW EXAMPLE
4. Credential Stuffing

When a major company has a data breach, hackers take the list of stolen emails and passwords and "stuff" them into other login pages (like Instagram or Banks) using automated bots.

> [Bot Script]: Running stolen_passwords.txt against gmail.com...

> Why it works: Most people use the exact same password for every site they visit.

▼ VIEW EXAMPLE
5. Dangerous Downloads

Downloading "cracked" games or free movies can hide a Trojan. This is a program that looks helpful but actually creates a "back door" for hackers to control your computer.

> File: Free_Game_Installer.exe

> Hidden payload: Remote_Access_Tool.dll

> Result: Hacker now has full control of your webcam and files.

▼ VIEW EXAMPLE
6. Social Media Over-sharing

Posting your location, your birthday, or your pet's name gives hackers the answers to your "Security Questions." They can use this info to reset your passwords without you knowing.

> Data Mining Social Profile...

> Answer found: High School = [MET High School]

> Answer found: Pet Name = [Morgana]

7. Text Message Scams (Smishing)

Getting a text saying "Your USPS package is delayed, click here" is a common scam. These links take you to fake sites designed to steal your credit card info.

> INCOMING SMS: "USPS: We have a package with no name. Visit usps-check-id.com to claim."

> Danger: The site looks 100% real but belongs to a thief.

▼ VIEW EXAMPLE
8. Identity Theft

This is the "End Game." Once a hacker has your name, social security number, or bank login, they can pretend to be you to take out loans or buy things in your name.

> Identity Status: COMPROMISED

> Consequences: Ruined credit, drained bank accounts, and years of legal work to fix.
